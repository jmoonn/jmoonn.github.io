    ticktBox is a third party platform for entertainment companies to promote screenings, events, and sweepstakes.


    ticktBox has their own platform, in which, entertainment companies can use to promote their sites at devgofobo.com. Companies simply insert their desired information to display what they want. This is an easy way for entertainment companies to do promote their offers. Big names such as Paramount Pictures, Sony Entertainment, Warner Bros, and Fox Searchlight use this platform. ticktBox also offers customizable platforms, in which, they go through their own personal host rather than devgofobo. Companies such as Broadgreen Pictures use this.


    For ticktBox, the first things I produced were wireframes for WB and Fox Searchlight. They were pages to promote their mobile applications. Developing these pages were my first time experiencing developing wireframes. Since they were the first wireframes I've produced, I learned a lot about developing wireframes. I learned about using a company toolbox kit, keeping conformity with the rest of the site, delivering the desired look and feel of a page. I also learned about grids and alignment. Since they are small, simple pages with relatively low importance, my supervisors were quite nice and guided me through the process. It was more of a project to test out my basic skills in design and get me comfortable with the process.

    Gofobo local is a sub-brand of ticktBox. Through gofobo local, users can find screenings, sweepstakes, and events in their local area. Gofobo local, still a work in progress, will be split up into 7 regions, across the nation. Through this, users will be able to easily find local events and also allows entertainment companies to better target their audience.


    Working at ticktBox, this project was given to me after I found the flow and rhythm of work. More than through my first works, I learned so much through gofobo. I had to come up with a color scheme, a vibe, and layout completely from scratch. Creating this sub-brand really humbled me and taught me I had so much more to learn. I thought I would breeze through this project easily. But I was taught through supervisors humility and constructive criticism. My supervisors were constantly helping me with things I had to fix. They reminded me to always continue to look for inspiration, ask for help, and start over multiple times.

    Through developing Gofobo Local, I learned about uniformity in iconography, typography, color scheme, and vibe. I also learned about the importance of constantly retrying and starting over, fixing things, finding new avenues, etc.

    Also through Gofobo Local, I was able to work with a team that was working to develop this sub-brand. I started by working with marketing, sales and PR. We developed a plan for how the site would function and what the site would offer. Starting with that, I developed basics and a skeleton of the design. From there, I worked with other deigners and supervisors to constantly re-design, edit, and come up with fresh ideas for the site. Simultaneously, I worked with developers to know what they could and could not do. Through Gofobo Local, I learned about teamwork and constant revamping.

    COGS187A is a course offered by UCSD to undergraduate students. It's a 6 unit class in usability and information architecture.



    This was my first class in HCI and design. Essentially, this class required a group (in our case, a group of 7), to create a wesbite from scratch. Our group created "idea8". This website took a google sprint called "Crazy Eights" and turned it digital. Crazy Eights is a designed sprint designed to make ideation quick, fast, and easy. It forces designers to spit something out, raw and unedited, by giving time constraints and panel requirements. Groups of designers then discuss their ideas and then do another iteration. Idea8 is a way to do this digitally, especially when members of a group cannot all meet at the same time, in real life.



    My group had a number of experienced UI/UX designers. They taught me an immense amount of information, from the basics of user testing all the way to the end of implementation. They taught me the absolute importance of user testing and data collection. Through them, I also learned not to skimp out on the ideation process. I learned that there could be a plethora of possibilites to solve a problem but settling on something too early on could limit my solution set and actually make design and implementation harder, in the long run.



    Together, we created color schemes, settled typographies, iconography, etc. Through this process, I learned about Color Theory and it's importance. I also learned about iconography and the idea of matching the vibe to the website.



    In terms of implementation, my HTML and CSS knowledge increased immensely. I was able to learn rules and how implementation, in general, worked. I learned about debugging, customization, stylesheets, templates, bootstrap, etc.



    This class was extremely informative and I learned a great deal from this class. It was, in my opinion, a class I grew exponentially in my knowledge of design in UI/UX.
