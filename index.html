ticktBox is an online marketing and ticketing platform. They work for some of the biggest names in the industry including Paramount Pictures, Sony Entertainment, Warner Bros., and Fox Searchlight. ticktBox provides a platform, usually using gofobo.com, or providing personalized sites, for these companies to host screenings, sweepstakes, and other events.

I started out by designing webpages to promote mobile apps for Warner Bros. and Fox Searchlight Screenings. Right now, I am currently working on creating wireframs for a subbrand of ticktBox called gofobo local. Gofobo local will narrow down searches for screenings, sweepstakes, and events to regional areas so users can more easily find things in their area.

When first working on it, I was confident and even arrogant. I started by myself and was confident my ideas were the best. But as I began to work on it, I realized user experience is extremely important, knowing statistics and data is important, and making it easier on the user is more important. Through discussions, brainstorming sessions, and advice from supervisors, I was able to create what I have now.

Right now, the final icon ideas and a couple of pages are still in the process of being developed. We are considering user experience, importance of content, and ease of access. I've also started to develop mobile versions of the finished wireframes. So far, this was one of my biggest learning experiences of the real world.

COGS187A is a 6 unit course offered to undergraduate students at UCSD. It is Usability and Information Architecture. Through this class, we work with a team to make a website that is both functional and aesthetically pleasing.

First, we started off with a general idea of what we wanted to come up with: creativity - something that enhances it. And then, we did a immense amount of user research. We asked what people thought of when thinking of creativity, how it developed, what problems they had with it, etc. After a number of meetings, organizations, crazy eights, wall walks, and a lot of sticky-notes, we came up with the idea of an online version of Crazy Eights.

Crazy Eights is a design sprint made for designers to spit out unpolished, raw ideas as fast as they can. It promotes ideation and creativity by forcing the designer to push something out. Then, members of the group discuss each other's ideas to come up with more ideas and another iteration of Crazy Eights is done to help come up with a final idea.

After coming up with this idea, we made low-fidelity prototypes. These prototypes were tested on fellow classmates, other students, friends, and even our professors! And through these prototypes, we made more high-fidelity ones with improved content, easier navigation, and improved user experience. Again, these high-fidelity prototypes were tested over and over again.
Soon, we will start implementation, including design and coding.

Through this course, I have learned an immense amount of information about UX/UI, especially through my more experienced teammates. They guided me a lot and helped explained a number of different processes. I've learned more about HTML, CSS, etc. than I ever have and learned about the importance of typography, iconography, and user experience design.
