THE OBJECTIVE

To create a website or an app that pushes an individual's creativity.


BACKGROUND INFORMATION

COGS187A is an upperdivision course, better known as Cognitive Aspects of Digital Design, offered at UCSD as an elective course to HCI and CSE students. In this class, students are required to work in groups of 4-6 to create a website or app of their choice while going through the UX design process, starting from user interviews to a semi-functional implementation.


THE PROCESS

User Interviews
After some contemplation, our group decided to tackle creativity. We weren't sure exactly what we wanted from this project and so we conducted user interviews about the general idea of creativity. These questions included questions about

  • what inspires you to be creative
  • how do you express your creativity
  • what do you do when you feel uninspired
  • what does creativity mean to you
  • & much more

Ideating, Evolving, and Iterating idea8
After consolidating our data, we looked it over, analyzed it and came to the conclusion that we wanted our users to be able to have an online platform to be able to force their creativity. Some additional features we also in mind were anonymity and collaborative work. And so, began the evolution of idea8 - an online website that would allow users to work by themselves or in groups to help begin a design/artwork/etc. It's an online spin of Google's design spring called Crazy 8's.

Crazy 8's asks designers to forcefully push out 8 (or more) ideas relating to their project in less than 8 minutes. Each idea is supposed to be rough, quick, and briefly written on one panel of paper each. Designers than share with each other and another round is conducted. It allows ideas to be built on each other and forces creativity by forcing designers to ignore constraining details. Idea8 was to be a digital version of this.



Personas, Storyboards, and High-Fidelity Prototypes
Having a better unstanding of what we wanted to create and who we wanted to create for, it was time to move onto personas, storyboards, and hi-fi prototypes. Based on user interviews, ideations, and iterations, we came to the following conclusions.
We were creating for (in order of importance):

  • groups (art, event, planning, any group, really) that needed a push getting started
  • groups that had difficulty finding a good time to meet
  • individuals trying to conquer creative blocks

We wanted idea8 to have these features:
  • online quick design ideas
  • chat
  • anonymity
  • sharing
  • saving projects

After these conclusions and a number of iterations of wireframes, we created high fidelity prototypes using Marvel App.



User Testing
We set up camp at our school's library, Geisel Library for a couple of hours on a weekend, asking bypassers to participate in trying out our app if they had the free time. Here are some conclusions:

  • the users were constantly confused about what exactly they were doing in each stage of the process
  • the settings were confusing and not sure why the defaults were set as those defaults
  • our layout was confusing and we needed to hash it out more

Final Conclusions
Idea8 was to be an online web app that pushed creativity within, most importantly, groups. It allowed group members to collaborate with each other remotely so that they could share, build, and consolidate ideas/designs. Since collaboration was essential, we decided that a chat feature was essential. In addition, it allowed individuals without groups to do the same by sharing their work with their friends. Some additional features were customization to give the user more freedom - they could choose a project name, description, number of "panels" to ideate on, and a set time for each panel.
